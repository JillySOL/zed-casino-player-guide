# Placing a Bet

## Bet Types

ZED supports single, multi, and exotic bets. Here they are at a glance. Tap any type below to expand the full detail.

| Bet Type | You Collect If... |
| --- | --- |
| Win | Your horse finishes 1st |
| Place | Your horse finishes in the top 3 |
| Multi | Every leg you pick wins |
| Trifecta | You pick the first three home |
| Supafecta | You pick the first four home |
| Dutch | Any horse you back in the race wins |

<details>

<summary><strong>Win</strong></summary>

Back a horse to finish 1st. The simplest bet on the board and the biggest return on a single runner: your payout is stake times odds, nothing else to it. Back the field's favourite and you pay for it in shorter odds. Fancy a live outsider and this is where the value sits.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Place</strong></summary>

Back a horse to finish in the top 3. The payout is smaller than a win bet because you've got three chances to collect instead of one, but that's the point. It's the bet for horses that run honest races without always saluting. Place odds are displayed alongside win odds on every runner, so you can compare before you commit. A lot of sharp players use place bets to back up their win selections: if the horse wins you collect both, if it runs a close second or third you still get something back.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Multi</strong></summary>

Stack multiple races into one bet and let the odds multiply. Pick a winner in each leg. Every leg that wins rolls your entire payout onto the next race. That compounding is where multis get dangerous (in a good way): a $5 multi across four legs at odds of 2.00, 3.00, 2.50 and 4.00 pays $5 × 2 × 3 × 2.5 × 4 = $300. The catch: all legs must win. One beaten favourite anywhere in the chain and the whole ticket is dead. With a race every 80 seconds, you can build a multi and watch every leg resolve inside five minutes.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Trifecta</strong></summary>

Pick the first three horses home, in exact finishing order. It's the hardest single-race bet on the board, which is why it consistently throws the biggest single-race payouts. Two ways to play it:

* **Straight.** Your three horses must finish 1st, 2nd, 3rd in the exact order you picked. One combination, one price.
* **Boxed.** Your three horses can finish in the top three in any order. Three horses boxed covers all 6 possible finishing orders, so it costs 6× a straight bet at the same unit stake, but any combination collects. You can also box 4 or more horses (4 horses = 24 combinations) to widen the net.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Supafecta</strong></summary>

The trifecta's big brother: nail the first four horses home in exact finishing order. In a 12-horse field there are 11,880 possible finishing orders for the first four, which is exactly why the payouts are massive. Play it straight for maximum return, or box four or more horses to cover every combination (4 horses boxed = 24 combos, 5 horses = 120). Most players attack it with a small unit stake across a wide box. This is the lottery ticket of the racing page: cheap to swing, huge when it lands.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Dutch</strong></summary>

Spread your stake across multiple horses in the same race so you profit the same amount no matter which one wins. Say you've narrowed a race down to three chances at odds of 3.00, 4.00 and 6.00. Instead of agonising over one, dutch all three. Enter your total stake and we split it automatically in proportion to the odds, so every winning outcome returns the same amount. Your effective odds are lower than backing one horse, but your strike rate goes way up. It's the bet for when you know who can't win but aren't sure who will.

</details>

## Bet Limits

| | Minimum Stake | Maximum Stake |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Per bet | 1 credit | 1,000 credits |

All limits are in USD-equivalent credits. For a boxed exotic, your stake is a **unit stake** and the total cost is that unit multiplied by the number of combinations (shown on the Bet Slip before you confirm). For a multi, you stake once and it rides through every leg.

## Step-by-Step: Place a Bet

1. From the Lobby or Race Detail page, find a race that's still open for betting.
2. Tap a horse in the runners list.
3. The Bet Slip opens (it floats over the page so you can keep browsing).
4. Choose your bet type. Win and Place settle on this race. For a Multi, Trifecta, Supafecta, or Dutch, keep adding selections (more legs, or more horses in the same race) and the Bet Slip builds the ticket for you.
5. Enter your stake (between 1 and 1,000 credits).
6. Confirm the potential return shown next to the stake.
7. Tap Place Bet.

The bet is now active. Your credit balance updates immediately.

## The Bet Slip

The Bet Slip is persistent. You can:

* Add multiple bets across different races before confirming.
* Adjust stakes line by line.
* Remove individual selections.
* See total stake and total potential return at the bottom.

## Fixed Odds

ZED uses fixed odds. Whatever odds you saw when you placed the bet are the odds you'll be paid out at — even if more bets come in afterward and shift the odds for other players. The house bears the risk.

## Cancelling a Bet

You can cancel a bet at any time before betting closes on that race.

1. Open the Bet Slip or Bet History.
2. Find your active bet.
3. Tap Cancel.
4. The full stake is refunded to your balance instantly.

Once betting closes (50 s into the race window), cancellation is no longer possible.
